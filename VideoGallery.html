The first hand understanding to the greatest album ever made by Joji.

Joji's top song in Nectar:

“Like You Do” finds Joji reflecting on a relationship through an emotional piano ballad. He sings about how his and his partner's dreams are leading them in different directions, and will inevitably end things between them. Joji is powerlessly in love with her, and without her, he feels incomplete.

On “MODUS”, Joji describes the pressures of the music industry as they try to mould him into a pop star. He feels tied down by those that he describes as being his “programmers.” The word MODUS might be a play to the Latin phrase “Modus Vivendi” meaning “an arrangement allowing people or groups of people who have different opinions or beliefs to work or live together.”

I don't feel the way they programmed me to feel today.

Joji knows that he is capable of achieving complete artistic freedom and strives to make music that he can feel completely proud of.

“Your Man,” described as a “pulsing ode to eyes-closed, four-on-the-floor escapism,” serves as the closing track for Nectar and sees Joji comfort his crush after an initial breakup between her and her previous lover.

My personal favorite song in his entire album

The song describes the aftermath of a dysfunctional relationship. Joji reflects on where he and his partner went wrong and wonders how the partner feels about the situation.

Despite a messy break-up, Joji does not bear his ex any ill will. He only prays that both of them will find happiness and solitude, and treasure the time they had together.
